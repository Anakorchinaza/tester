OUR MISSION
At GMA we are dedicated to providing an excellent education in a caring, respectful environment, challenging students to achieve their full potential as successful global citizens. AIMS

  • To provide an internationally recognised curriculum.
  • To encourage global citizenship, providing students with the knowledge, skills and attitudes to participate positively in the world.
  • To provide a range of opportunities that challenge students to develop a wide range of skills.
  • To ensure a safe, secure and well-equipped learning environment for all.

SCHOOL HISTORY
Today Great Mind Academy has over 470 students from over 12 nationalities and follows the National Curriculum Programme of Study for England. It is the oldest international school in Nigeria with a well-deserved reputation for delivering a high quality education programme for over 10 years. The school was originally established in the 1990’s to provide a British type education for children of expatriates living and working in Nigeria. Initially, premises were rented from the St John’s Ambulance Association and the first teachers were parents of the students attending the school. However, the school quickly introduced its current policy of recruiting only the best qualified teachers from around the world and began to receive recognition of its commitment to high standards from local, regional and global educational organisations.

To provide every learner with opportunities for educational engagements that foster the development of the individual’s self-image, social competence, spiritual consciousness, intellectual and occupational skills acquisition…as a springboard for personal goal achievement, and full integration into the larger society.

In 2000, starting a period of sustained academic success which has continued through to the present time with results comparable to the best Schools around the world. The WAEC programme was quickly resourced by the building of a dedicated secondary department block of classrooms, complete with science laboratories in 2001. The year 2001 also witnessed the creation of an ICT Laboratory to ensure that students have every opportunity to develop high levels of computer literacy.
